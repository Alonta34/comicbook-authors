The Man Behind the Heroes

Stanley Martin Lieber or better known as Stan Lee was an amazing comic book artist responsible for most of your favorite comic book superheroes that come from Marvel Comics. Some of these heroes, you no doubtably already know: Spider-Man, Hulk, Thor, and even Iron man. He was a truly innovative force in the world of pop culture in a time where things like comic books were at a bit of a low. Here is a look into some of the characters that Stan Lee took part in creating.

Black Panther

The king of the Wakanda, T’Challa is the Black Panther. First introduced in Fantastic Four #52 July 1966. Later, in 1977 he got his own comic line. He is one of the most intelligent characters in the Marvel universe being well versed in multiple languages, sciences, mathematics, fighting disciplines, and even magic. The Black Panther is an invaluable member of the Avengers but the same is also true of his kingdom, which can make things difficult at times. For example when it comes to helping the Avengers or tending to the needs of his country, his country under no circumstance will ever be secondary.

Storm

Although she was not created by Stan Lee, Storm is an example on how influential Stan Lee’s works were. With the revamping of the once retired X-Men comic created by Stan Lee and Jack Kirby, now dubbed Giant Size X-Men in 1975 Storm made her debut in the first issue. A powerful black woman who was born in Africa, She has the ability to control the weather and the power of flight. She is easily one of the most powerful X-Men and was even the sole leader for quite some time. At one point she was even married to T’Challa (Black Panther) making her the queen of Wakanda.

Spider-Man

Spider-Man is undoubtedly Stan Lee’s most popular character ever created. Alongside Steve Ditko, Stan was able to bring this character to life in 1962 in Amazing Fantasy #15. Although most of their superiors wanted Stan and Steve to make the character out to be like the other heroes before him, a middle aged man, Stan and Steve stayed true to their ambitions and wrote him as a teenager that struggled with everyday life and thus created one the most iconic comic book characters ever. Our favorite wall-crawler has truly stood the test of time with countless stories and iterations, with Miles Morales possibly being the most popular.
