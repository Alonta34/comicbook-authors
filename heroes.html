The king of the Wakanda, T’Challa is the Black Panther. First introduced in Fantastic Four #52 July 1966. Later, in 1977 he got his own comic line. He is one of the most intelligent characters in the Marvel universe being well versed in multiple languages, sciences, mathematics, fighting disciplines, and even magic. The Black Panther is an invaluable member of the Avengers but the same is also true of his kingdom, which can make things difficult at times. For example when it comes to helping the Avengers or tending to the needs of his country, his country under no circumstance will ever be secondary.

Spider-Man is undoubtedly Stan Lee’s most popular character ever created. Alongside Steve Ditko, Stan was able to bring this character to life in 1962 in Amazing Fantasy #15. Although most of their superiors wanted Stan and Steve to make the character out to be like the other heroes before him, a middle aged man, Stan and Steve stayed true to their ambitions and wrote him as a teenager that struggled with everyday life and thus created one the most iconic comic book characters ever. Our favorite wall-crawler has truly stood the test of time with countless stories and iterations, with Miles Morales possibly being the most popular.

One of Captain America’s closest friends, Sam Wilson better known as the Falcon is Captain America’s right hand man. Before he was the Falcon Sam was just a regular guy trying to make ends meet in the streets of Chicago. But through a series of events he was able to don the mantle of the Falcon and eventually the shield of Captain America, becoming the face of the shield himself. With his high tech wings and suit, Falcon can fly at high speeds and altitudes and is also able to control a remote control drone that he has dubbed the name Redwing. He First appeared in Captain America #117 in September 1969 eventually making his debut as Captain America himself in All-New Captain America in November 2014.

Although she was not created by Stan Lee, Storm is an example on how influential Stan Lee’s works were. With the revamping of the once retired X-Men comic created by Stan Lee and Jack Kirby, now dubbed Giant Size X-Men in 1975 Storm made her debut in the first issue. A powerful black woman who was born in Africa, She has the ability to control the weather and the power of flight. She is easily one of the most powerful X-Men and was even the sole leader for quite some time. At one point she was even married to T’Challa (Black Panther) making her the queen of Wakanda.

Magneto, born Max Eisenhardt but later changing his name to Erik Lehsherr is the most recurring archenemy of the X-Men but has steadily been written to be more of an adversary to X-Men. He is an anti-hero of sorts. Magneto is the founder of the mutant team known as the Brotherhood and together they are the foil to the X-Men’s ideals of peace between mutants and humans by the means of nonviolence. He is the master of magnetism, being able to control and create any and all magnetic fields. Stan Lee and Jack Kirby’s idea for Magneto was to make him akin to the Malcom X with his willingness to use violence to bring about peace between mutants and humans. They did not view him as the “bad guy” but as someone who only wanted equality for his people. Introduced in The X-Men #1 in September 1963 he is probably the longest recurring adversary for the X-Men.

Making his appearance with the rest of the team in The Fantastic Four #1 in November 1961, Johnny Storm aka the Human Torch is basically the child of the Fantastic Four. He’s impulsive, wild, and always living on the edge and it shows in the powers that he got in the cosmic accident he went through with the rest of the Fantastic Four. He is best friends with Bobby Drake (Iceman) and Peter Parker (Spider-Man) always causing trouble with his pranks. But when it's time to get serious, Johnny can always be counted on to do what needs to be done. The Human Torch is able to set himself on fire at will, is able to fly, and can shoot fire projectiles.

Tony Stark is the Invincible Ironman, albeit not as invincible as you would think. Born Anthony Howard Stark, Ironman is one of the smartest people in marvel specializing in business and engineering but at the same he is his own worst enemy. For many years even before donning the high tech suit of Ironman he struggled with depression and alcoholism but eventually found a way to beat both eventually becoming the version of Ironman you see today in movies. Tony has a massive collection of Ironman suits built for literally any occasion. Making his first appearance in Tales of Suspense #39 in March 1963 and eventually got his own comic in May 1968.

Another example on how the works of Stan Lee led to great creations from other comic book artists. James Rhodes (Rhodey), first introduced in Iron Man #118 in January 1979, is Ironman’s most trusted friend. So much so that Tony himself offered the mantle of Ironman to Rhodey when he couldn't continue. Of course Rhodey took up the mantle when his best friend couldn’t perform and was great at it. Tony wound up creating a suit of armor specifically designed for Rhodey. This high tech heavy weapons focused suit would become Rhodey’s staple armor and everyone would come to know him as the War Machine, one of the few people who is able to keep Ironman himself in check. His suit has repulsor beams just like Ironman but unlike Ironman, War Machine's suit holds numerous heavy weapons like machine guns, missiles,and a shoulder mounted heavy caliber rifle that doubles as a rocket launcher.

The “cool dude” of the original team of X-Men. Iceman (Robert Drake) has stayed the same more or less up until recently in comics. He’s one of the best friends of the Human Torch and is a potential power house member of the X-Men, should he ever be able to reach his potential. He has the ability to form any object he can imagine out of ice which includes a huge ice slide that he can use for long distance traversal, that can be made in mid air might I add. He can freeze the atmosphere around and within him turning his body into solid ice. This allows him to tank heavy hits and regenerate lost limbs with water particles in the air around him. Despite his depiction as one of the younger X-Men his is actually one of the founding members of the original team, making his first appearance in The X-Men #1 in September 1963.

Doctor Stephen Strange, master of the mystic arts has a funny history when it comes to magic. Being a doctor who specialized who specialized in neurosurgery, magic was never on the forefront of his mind. That is until a freak accident took away the use of his hands. Without the use of his hands he couldn’t perform his practice, and his practice was all he had devoted his life to. When all the science in the world couldn’t help him fully recover, Stephen Strange turned to the mystic arts. After years of training and commitment, Doctor Strange the surgeon became Doctor Strange the sorcerer supreme. Specializing is just about every aspect of the mystic arts the best question is what is it he can’t do. Introduced in Strange Tales #110 in July 1963, Doctor Strange has been a sort of background character until recently.
